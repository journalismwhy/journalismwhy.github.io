TRAVELING JOURNALISM

Privacy policy

Your privacy on the Internet is of utmost importance to Journalism Why. This privacy statement describes Journalism Why’s policy concerning the gathering and sharing of visitors’ information through the Journalism Why web site. It applies to all “Journalism Why sites” – that is, all sites within the “journalismwhy.com” domain name.

What information does Journalism Why collect?

Normal web site usage

In general, you can browse the Journalism Why site without telling us who you are or revealing any personal information about yourself. The only information we gather during general browsing is from standard server logs. These include your IP (Internet Protocol) address, domain name, browser type, operating system, and information such as the web site that referred you to us, the files you download, the pages you visit, and the dates/times of those visits.

Collection of personally identifiable information

If you use our contact form, you will be asked to provide personal information such as your name and e-mail address.

This information is collected only with your knowledge and permission and is kept in various Journalism Why databases and mailing lists. If you are purchasing something using the web, you may be asked to provide your credit card details. These details are transferred to a secure, online payment service, hosted outside Journalism Why. Credit card information is not retained by Journalism Why.

Registering or supplying personal information, like using our contact form, sets a cookie*. By setting this cookie, Journalism Why will remember your details the next time you visit, so you don’t have to re-enter the information. This helps us to provide you with a better service.

What does Journalism Why do with the information it collects?

Normal web site usage

The information gathered during general browsing of the “journalismwhy.com” domain is used to analyze trends and usage of the Journalism Why site and to improve the usefulness of the site. It is not connected with any personal information.

Personally identifiable information

Journalism Why may use the personal information you provide to:

  • Contact you – either in response to a query or suggestion, or to mail newsletters, documents, publications etc. to you;
  • Help you quickly find information that is relevant to you based on your interests, and help us create site content most relevant to you;
  • Statistical analysis.

What if I don’t want to provide personal information?

Providing personal information on the Journalism Why site is optional. If you choose not to provide personal information, you can still browse and use the Journalism Why site, but you will not be able to carry out certain actions.

Opting out and changing your information

At any time, you can cancel or modify your information by returning to the web page where you first provided the information and contacting us.

Security

We do not sell or share any personally identifiable information volunteered on the Journalism Why site to any third party. Any information provided to Journalism Why by users of the Journalism Why site is held with the utmost care and security, and will not be used in ways other than as set forth in this privacy policy, or in any site-specific policies, or in ways to which you have explicitly consented. Journalism Why employs a range of technologies and security measures to protect the information maintained on our systems from loss, misuse, unauthorized access or disclosure, alteration, or destruction.

We respect the confidentiality of business matters, including personal data.

The Journalism Why site contains links to sites external to the journalismwhy.com domain. Journalism Why is not responsible for the privacy practices or the content of such sites.

Notification of changes

Changes to this privacy policy will be posted on the Journalism Why home page at www.journalismwhy.com.

Contact

For questions or queries regarding this privacy policy, please contact us here.

*Cookies

A cookie is a small amount of data that is sent from a web server to your browser. It is normally used to assign a unique identification to your computer and securely store information such as user IDs, passwords, preferences, and online profiles.

It is stored on the hard drive of your computer. You can choose not to have cookies delivered by Journalism Why sites by changing your browser settings. Different web sites can send their own cookies to your computer. To protect your privacy, your browser only allows a web site to access the cookies it has already sent you, not the cookies sent by other sites.

You can change your browser's settings in order to disallow the use of cookies, but this action might have negative impacts on your browsing experience.
